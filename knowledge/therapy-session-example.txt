KEY LESSONS FROM THIS SESSION

1. TIMING
- Waited 9+ minutes (00:09:51) before key symbolic intervention
- Gathered extensive narrative before mapping
- Intervention came when D had fully articulated the violin/precision pattern
- Readiness indicators: D made his own connection (cameras → violins), was discussing the pattern openly

2. SYMBOLIC MAPPING
- Present pattern: Demands precision/perfection from partners before relationship develops
- Historical source: Mother's rigid precision demands with violin; father's violence (broken hand)
- Symbolic bridge used: "So what if you replace the violin with a partner?"
- Why this connection worked: D had already been thinking symbolically (cameras = violins); the pattern was fully articulated; the intervention was simple and direct

3. STRATEGIC PATIENCE
- Did NOT interpret prematurely at 00:04:09 when precision theme emerged
- Did NOT name the pattern explicitly until D was ready
- Did NOT push for affect - let broken hand emerge naturally
- Held 42 seconds of silence before Thend moment

4. THEND MOMENT (00:20:45)
- Question: "How do you get out from under his curse?"
- Answer: "I get from under the curse, his curse, by really living a life that I enjoy."
- This is operational mastery - D can now WORK WITH the contradiction rather than being controlled by it
- The curse doesn't disappear; D gains control over his relationship to it

5. REGISTER INTEGRATION
- Real: Broken hand (physical trauma)
- Imaginary: Ongoing consequences, expectations, perfectionism
- Symbolic: The meaning - father's curse, mother's precision, what the hand represents
- Integration: All three registers named and connected at 00:17:13

6. WHAT NOT TO DO
- Premature interpretation at 00:04:09 would have foreclosed D's own elaboration
- Forcing the partner connection before 00:09:51 would have created resistance
- Explaining the symbolism rather than letting D feel it would have intellectualized away the affect
- Rushing past the broken hand would have missed the trauma root

7. INDICATORS OF READINESS
- D spontaneously connected domains (cameras → violins)
- D identified the direction problem (precision first vs. love first)
- D was speaking in symbolic terms naturally
- D's narrative was approaching the traumatic material organically
- Physical symptoms (hand twitching) were being discussed openly
